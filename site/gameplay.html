Core Gameplay Pillars

  • Survive: Gather resources, build shelter, and protect your tribe from threats and the environment.
    // GameMaker concept: Use objects for resources and structures. Pops can have a needs system (hunger, thirst, etc.) and interact with the environment using state machines.
  • Evolve: Earn Evolution Points (EP) to unlock new traits and shape your lineage. Traits are inherited and can mutate, driving diversity.
    // Educational: Implement a trait tree using data structures (ds_maps, ds_lists) and random mutation logic for emergent gameplay.
  • Branch: Guide tribe splits and adaptation, creating new cultures and legacies. Tribes can specialize based on their environment.
    // Game design: Use arrays or ds_lists to manage multiple tribes and their unique properties. This encourages replayability and player-driven stories.
  • Legacy: Track family trees, milestones, and traditions as your civilization grows. Pops have life cycles, relationships, and can pass down knowledge.
    // GameMaker tip: Store pop data in structs/objects and use lists to track family trees and key events.

Key Mechanics

  • Needs & FSM: Pops use a finite state machine (FSM) to decide actions (idle, forage, hunt, craft, socialize, etc.) based on their needs and environment.
  • Resource & Inventory System: Each pop has a personal inventory, and the tribe can build communal storage. Items include food, materials, tools, and crafted goods.
  • Crafting & Technology: Unlock new recipes and blueprints through discovery points (DP) and "Eureka!" moments. Pops improve skills by crafting and can create higher-quality items.
  • Dynamic Environment: Multiple biomes, weather, seasons, and hazards affect gameplay. Resource nodes regrow, and animal behavior is simulated for realism.
  • Social & Tribal Dynamics: Pops form relationships, develop moods, and can become leaders. Traditions and customs emerge, affecting gameplay and tribe cohesion.
